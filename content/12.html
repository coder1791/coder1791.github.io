Why dinosaurs are extinct

There are multiple theories as to why dinosaurs went extinct, but the most widely accepted theory is that an asteroid struck the Earth approximately 66 million years ago. This event caused widespread devastation, including wildfires, tsunamis, and a long-lasting winter. The combination of these factors led to the extinction of the dinosaurs, as well as many other species.

However, it's important to note that not all dinosaurs went extinct. Birds, which are considered to be living dinosaurs, survived and continue to thrive today. Additionally, some non-avian dinosaurs, such as crocodiles, also survived the mass extinction event.

While the asteroid impact is the most accepted theory for the extinction of the dinosaurs, there are also other contributing factors that may have played a role. These include volcanic activity, climate change, and disease.
